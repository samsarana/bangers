About Book of Bangers

Book of Bangers is a quiet reading room for some of the best writing from TPOT — This Part of Twitter — a loose network of accounts that have been thinking out loud together for the better part of a decade. It is an anthology, not a feed. Browse it the way you would a printed collection: pick a metric, scroll until something catches you, sit with it.

Where the data comes from

Every tweet here is drawn from the Community Archive — an open, public-domain archive of TPOT-adjacent accounts initiated by @exgenesis (xiq). The snapshot used for this site was taken in May 2026 and covers around 340 archived accounts. Counts shown are point-in-time at the moment of the snapshot.

The three metrics

Likes, retweets and quote tweets each surface a different kind of tweet. They are not interchangeable.

  • Likes — tweets people wanted to see more of. The broadest signal of resonance, and a direct input to each user's algorithmic feed.
  • Retweets — tweets worth signal-boosting. An explicit, public endorsement to one's followers.
  • Quote tweets — tweets people wanted to respond to or build on. The noisiest of the three: it captures generativity, but also disagreement, and tweets that explicitly ask for QTs.

Empirically, likes and retweets correlate strongly across this corpus (Pearson 0.81); both are nearly uncorrelated with quote tweet count (0.10 and 0.09). The three lists overlap less than you might expect — quote tweets in particular surface a distinctly different slice of the archive.

About the QT metric in particular

Quote tweet count tracks notable enough to respond to publicly. That overlaps substantially with worth including in an anthology of insight, but the two are not identical. Expect to find ideas generative enough to inspire responses, but also things that provoked enough disagreement to warrant a public rebuttal, and the occasional "quote this with your…" prompt.

Caveats

  • Counts are a snapshot, not live.
  • QT counts are corpus-internal lower bounds: they only count quote tweets present in the archive itself.
  • Self-quotes are excluded from QT counts.
  • Retweets ("RT @…") by archive accounts are excluded from the primary set; only original tweets and replies are ranked.

Display options

Two toggles in the header. Reading mode hides avatars, names and dates, leaving only the text and the metrics — useful if you want to read without the social layer. The links toggle hides every outbound link to x.com and renders in-text URLs as plain text, so the anthology stays self-contained.
